With over 170,000 hectares of vineyard stretched throughout four major Australian wine regions- Western Australia, New South Wales, Victoria, and South Australia- Australia was able to secure a spot as the top 7th among the world's top wine-producing nations. Each region offers a varieties of wines and wine tourism experiences. Asutralia's wine sector emphasises on their variety of grapes unlike other countries where their wines are an expression of the specific soil and climate of the country.

Wine Regions

From the map, we are able to see that Victoria and New South Wales cover a larger area for the land used for vineyards. However, in terms of production, South Australia has the largest amount of wine produced, with Victoria and New South Wales coming in close second and third for wine production.

Wine Production

The total production volume shows fluctuations throughout the years, indicating variability in wine production capacity or yield perhaps due to economical factors or environemntal factors. From the graph, we can see that there's a higher prodution volume of red wine due to its popularity over the recent years. Moreover, in terms of grape variety, most vineyards seems to produce wine using 'Shiraz', 'Chardonnay', and 'Cabernet Sauvignon'grapes.
